🎯 What Does Aircraft Air Conditioning Do? Core
▾

Aircraft A/C systems manage cabin air for comfort, safety, and pressurization.

🌡️ Cool

Reduces hot bleed or ambient air to comfortable cabin temperature.

💨 Airflow

Controls the quantity and distribution of conditioned air.

💧 Moisture

Removes or manages humidity inside the cabin.

🔒 Pressurization

Air cycle systems can support cabin pressurization on turbine aircraft.

⚡
Quick Recap: A/C systems cool, circulate, dehumidify, and — on turbine aircraft — support pressurization of cabin air.

🌀 Air Cycle Air Conditioning ATA 21
▾

An open system that takes hot bleed air, cools it through compression and expansion, removes moisture, and delivers conditioned air to the cabin.

💡
Definition: The air cycle system (also called the air conditioning pack) uses bleed air from the engine compressor, processes it through heat exchangers and an ACM, and supplies cooled, dehumidified air.

Air Cycle System Flow Sequence

  1. Bleed Air — Hot, pressurized air extracted from engine compressor.
  2. Primary Heat Exchanger — Ram air cools bleed air (acts like a radiator).
  3. ACM Compressor — Air enters the ACM; compression raises temperature again.
  4. Secondary Heat Exchanger — Ram air cools the compressed air again.
  5. ACM Turbine — Air expands → large temperature drop → air becomes very cold.
  6. Water Separator — Removes condensed moisture from cold, saturated air.
  7. Mix & Deliver — Warm bypass air mixes with cold conditioned air → correct cabin temperature.
🧠
Memory Line: Cool → Compress → Cool Again → Expand → Dry → Mix → Deliver

🔧 Air Cycle Main Components Detail
▾

🔁 Air Cycle Machine (ACM)

  • Compressor + turbine on one shaft
  • Produces the major cooling effect
  • Heart of the air cycle system

🚪 Pack Valve

  • Controls bleed air entering the pack
  • Opens, closes, and modulates flow
  • Auto-closes on overheat
  • Also called: supply shutoff valve

🔀 Mixing Valve

  • Routes warm air around cooling section
  • Mixes warm + cold air for temp control
  • Also called: trim air valve / temperature control valve

🌡️ Heat Exchangers

  • Primary: first-stage cooling via ram air
  • Secondary: cools ACM-compressed air
  • Ground: fan assists airflow
  • Flight: ram air doors regulate flow

💧 Water Separator

  • Removes moisture from cold air
  • Prevents water entering cabin
  • Usually no moving parts
  • Air swirls → water drains out

🧊 Refrigeration Bypass Valve

  • Prevents water separator freezing
  • Adds warm bypass air upstream
  • Also called: anti-ice valve
⚠️
Exam Point: The ACM is the heart of the air cycle system. The pack valve automatically closes during overheat or abnormal conditions.
💨 Pneumatic Supply
▾

Bleed air source and typical parameters for the air cycle system.

Source

Typical Pressure

Supplies

  • Air conditioning packs
  • Anti-ice systems
  • Hydraulic pressurization
  • Other pneumatic systems
❄️ Vapor Cycle Air Conditioning Key Concept
▾

A closed refrigerant system that moves heat out of the cabin — it does not "create cold."

💡
Core Principle: Vapor cycle systems do not create cold — they move heat from inside the cabin to outside the aircraft using a refrigerant loop.

Vapor Cycle Flow Sequence

  1. Receiver Dryer — Stores refrigerant, filters contaminants, removes moisture.
  2. Expansion Valve — Meters refrigerant into evaporator; causes pressure drop.
  3. Evaporator — Refrigerant absorbs cabin heat; liquid changes to vapor; cabin air cools.
  4. Compressor — Compresses low-pressure vapor; raises pressure and temperature.
  5. Condenser — Rejects heat to outside air; vapor condenses back to liquid.
  6. Back to Receiver Dryer — Cycle repeats continuously.
🧠
Memory Line: Meter → Evaporate → Compress → Condense → Store → Repeat

Refrigerant Types

🔴 R12 (Old)

  • Harmful to ozone layer
  • No longer commonly used

🟢 R134a (Modern)

  • Boiling point ≈ −26°C
  • Environmentally safer than R12
  • Can displace oxygen
  • Can cause frost burns
⚠️
CRITICAL: NEVER mix R12 and R134a. Always use the refrigerant specified for the system. Use gloves and eye protection when handling refrigerant.
🔩 Vapor Cycle Main Components Detail
▾

⚙️ Compressor — Heart of Vapor Cycle

  • Circulates refrigerant
  • Compresses low-pressure vapor
  • Raises temperature + sends to condenser
  • Must compress vapor only — never liquid
  • Types: engine-driven / electric / hydraulic

🫙 Receiver Dryer

  • Stores liquid refrigerant
  • Removes moisture (desiccant)
  • Filters contaminants
  • Sight glass — bubbles = low refrigerant

🔧 Expansion Valve

  • Controls refrigerant flow into evaporator
  • Too little → excessive superheat, poor cooling
  • Too much → liquid reaches compressor → damage

🌡️ Evaporator

  • Absorbs heat from cabin air
  • Liquid changes to low-pressure vapor
  • Where cabin heat is absorbed

🌬️ Condenser

  • Rejects heat to outside air
  • Vapor → liquid
  • Where heat leaves the aircraft

🔌 Service Valves

  • Access for servicing high + low sides
  • Used for charging refrigerant
  • Used for system maintenance
⚠️
Moisture in Vapor Cycle: Moisture can form acid, cause corrosion, or freeze and block the system. The receiver dryer removes it using desiccant.

🔬 Refrigeration Theory Theory
▾

🌡️ Heat

A form of energy. Always flows hot → cold.

🧊 Latent Heat

Heat absorbed or released during a change of state. Temperature stays constant during state change.

💨 Superheat

Heating vapor after all liquid has evaporated.

🔄 Boiling Point

Changes with pressure. Higher pressure → higher boiling point. Lower pressure → lower boiling point.

📦 Vapor Pressure

Pressure of vapor above a liquid in a closed container. Depends on substance type and temperature.

⚡
Exam Key: Vapor cycle systems exploit latent heat. Refrigerant boiling point is lowered by the expansion valve (pressure drop) to absorb cabin heat.
🎛️ Flow, Temperature & Humidity Control Systems
▾

Flow Control

Engine RPM, altitude, and air density all change during flight — flow control keeps cabin airflow constant.

💡
A mass airflow controller maintains constant cabin airflow despite changing bleed air pressure and density.

Temperature Control — 5-Step Process

  1. Crew Selects desired temperature from cockpit panel.
  2. Sensors Monitor cabin, cockpit, conditioned air duct, and distribution duct temperatures.
  3. Controller Compares actual vs. selected temperature.
  4. Valve Controlled — signals mixing valve, trim air valve, or temperature control valve.
  5. Warm + Cold Air Mix → correct final cabin supply temperature.

Humidity Control

💧 Moisture Removal

Water separators in the air cycle system remove excess moisture from conditioned air.

💦 Humidification

At high altitude cabin air may become too dry. Some aircraft pump water from a tank and spray moisture into conditioned air, monitored electronically.

🚨 Safety & Warning Systems Safety-Critical
▾
⚠️
Main Hazards:
  • Overheating — Hot pneumatic duct leaks can become fire hazards.
  • Pack Overheat — Excessive outlet temp may damage the system.
  • Smoke — May indicate fire or system malfunction.

Protection Devices

🔁 Continuous Loop Detectors

  • Detect heat along pneumatic ducts
  • Installed around ducts, APU, A/C bays

🌡️ Thermal Switches

  • Monitor pack outlet temperature
  • Auto-shutdown on overheat

🚪 Shutoff / Isolation Valves

  • Isolate faulty pneumatic sections
  • Allow continued safe operation

🔔 Warning Annunciations

  • Cockpit warnings for: overheat, smoke, pneumatic leaks

Question
What is the heart of the air cycle system?
Answer
The Air Cycle Machine (ACM) — compressor and turbine on a common shaft.
Question
What does the water separator do?
Answer
Removes condensed moisture from cold, saturated air before cabin delivery. Usually has no moving parts.
Question
What refrigerant is used in modern vapor cycle systems?
Answer
R134a — boiling point ≈ −26°C. Environmentally safer than R12.
Question
Where does heat absorption occur in the vapor cycle?
Answer
At the Evaporator — refrigerant absorbs cabin heat and changes from liquid to vapor.
Question
Where does heat leave the aircraft in the vapor cycle?
Answer
At the Condenser — vapor condenses back to liquid and heat is rejected to outside air.
Question
What components divide the high and low sides?
Answer
The Compressor (high side start) and the Expansion Valve (low side start).
Question
What is the pack valve also called?
Answer
Supply shutoff valve. Automatically closes during overheat or abnormal conditions.
Question
Can the vapor cycle system pressurize the cabin?
Answer
No — it is a closed system used for cooling only. Only air cycle systems can support pressurization.
Question
What is latent heat?
Answer
Heat absorbed or released during a change of state — temperature remains constant during the change.
Question
What is the refrigeration bypass valve also called?
Answer
Anti-ice valve or temperature control valve. Prevents water separator freezing.
